The Capability

I Built the
System That
Runs the Work.

Every sports organization I've worked with faces the same production problem: the calendar never stops, the demand for content never stops, and the budget for headcount always has a ceiling. The answer isn't to hire more people. It's to build smarter infrastructure.

I've spent three years building and refining AI-augmented production workflows that allow a lean creative team to produce at the volume and quality of a much larger operation. This isn't about replacing creativity with automation. It's about removing the friction between creative direction and finished output — so the human energy goes where it matters most.

The system I built is deployed across La Liga Israel, Winner League, and other clients. It's the reason we produced over 1,000 pieces of content per month at La Liga Israel with a small team. It's the reason our engagement metrics outperformed organizations running teams three times our size. And it's a capability I bring to any senior creative leadership role.

The Design Principle

"AI doesn't replace creative direction. It removes the bottleneck between the idea and the asset — so the director can direct more, not produce less."

— How I position AI in every production workflow I build.

What I Actually Do

The Director's
Role in an AI
Pipeline

01

Prompt System Architecture

I design the prompt infrastructure for each client — not one-off prompts, but reusable, parameterized prompt systems that encode the brand's visual DNA. A well-built prompt library means every team member can generate on-brand assets without requiring senior art direction on each output. I build, maintain, and update these systems as brand guidelines evolve.

02

Quality Gate Design

The most important structural decision in any AI pipeline is where the human review points sit. I design quality gates that are tight enough to catch off-brand output and fast enough to maintain production velocity. For La Liga Israel, I built a three-stage review process that kept brand compliance at 100% while adding less than 15 minutes to each asset's production cycle.

03

AI Tool Integration

I work across Claude, ChatGPT, and Claude Code for prompt architecture, copy, and workflow automation, and Higgsfield across its full range — image, video, and everything in between — for generation. I evaluate new tools against specific production needs rather than adopting for novelty — and I know when a manual process is faster.

04

Team Training & Workflow Documentation

An AI workflow that only I can operate isn't a system — it's a dependency. I build every pipeline to be teachable and documented. Junior team members can produce senior-level output through the guardrails I build. This is the multiplier effect: one well-designed system scales the capability of every person who uses it, permanently.

05

Performance Analysis & Iteration

I track which AI-assisted content formats perform against which manual formats — and use that data to refine both the prompt systems and the editorial criteria. The La Liga Israel engagement data didn't just validate the workflow; it showed me exactly which types of AI-assisted content were outperforming manual production and why. That learning loop is how the system improves continuously rather than becoming stale.

What I've Learned

AI Changes
the Job,
Not the Role.

Three years of building AI production systems across sports clients has taught me more about creative direction than it has about AI. The technology is a tool. The judgment about when to use it, how to constrain it, and what it cannot replace — that's the work.

The Brief Is More Important Than the Tool

AI is only as good as the creative direction it receives. A weak brief produces unusable AI output regardless of which model you use. The investment in clear, specific, brand-anchored creative direction pays compound returns in an AI pipeline — because it runs at 10× the volume of a manual one.

Quality Gates Are the Whole Game

The organizations that get AI production wrong skip the review stage. They assume AI output is good enough because it passes a visual check. The organizations that get it right build systematic quality gates — specific criteria, applied consistently, by humans who know the brand deeply. I design those gates before I design any prompt.

The CD's Value Goes Up, Not Down

The most common fear I hear from creative directors about AI: that it makes their role redundant. My experience is the opposite. When production is fast, the bottleneck shifts to creative judgment — which is exactly what a CD provides. AI doesn't replace the person who decides what's worth making. It makes that decision more consequential.
