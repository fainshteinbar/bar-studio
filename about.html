The Decision

Sports.
Not a vertical.
A choice.

My first client in 2019 was a sports organization. Every client since has been a sports organization. That's not an accident — it's a deliberate choice I made early and have never reconsidered.

Most creative directors come to sports from general agencies. They've built portfolios across fashion, finance, FMCG, and tech — and sports is one more vertical they're applying skills to. I've never had a non-sports client. That means I'm not learning the industry on your brief. I already understand the tension between heritage and disruption, the emotional relationship between fans and clubs, and the operational reality of working with federations that have complex stakeholder structures.

When La Liga Israel needed a creative director who could localize a global brand without diluting it, that understanding was worth more than any generalist portfolio. When the Israel Football Association needed a brand system that could sit beside UEFA and FIFA — not just look inspired by them — that specificity was the difference. Sports clients don't need to explain what they do to me. We start at the work.

Creative Philosophy

Three principles.
Every project.

01
Design for the fan at 0.5 seconds

A fan scrolling through their feed doesn't pause to read. They feel. The question I ask before every design decision is: what does this communicate before a single word is read? If the answer isn't clear in half a second, the work isn't done.

02
Build systems, not seasons

The best sports creative work outlasts the campaign it was built for. I design brand architectures — template systems, visual languages, scalable identity frameworks — that make every piece of content downstream cheaper, faster, and more consistent to produce. One season's work that sets up the next five is worth ten seasons of one-offs.

03
Earned authority over borrowed hype

Sports organizations are tempted by trend-chasing — whatever the biggest club or league is doing this season. The brands that last are built on earned authority: clear positioning, restrained visual language, and a consistent point of view that fans can recognize before they read the logo. I build for durability, not virality.

AI Production

The AI production conversation in sports media is mostly noise. Most organizations are experimenting with tools — running images through Midjourney, using GPT to write captions. That's not a workflow. I built an end-to-end production infrastructure: prompting logic, quality gates, brand compliance checks, and human review at every stage that matters. The result is a system that produced 10× the content volume of a traditional team — with zero drop in quality metrics.
